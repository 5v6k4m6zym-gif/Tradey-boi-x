Tradey Boi X – Safe Profit Factor Optimisation (Target: 1.40+)

Objective

Optimise Tradey Boi X to improve its long-term profit factor from approximately 1.21 toward 1.40 or higher, while preserving the existing architecture, strategy, alerting system, and overall reliability.

Core Requirements

* Do not rewrite the bot from scratch.
* Do not remove or disable any existing functionality.
* Preserve all current buy/sell alert behaviour unless an improvement is demonstrably beneficial.
* Maintain backwards compatibility with every existing feature.
* Optimise only where there is measurable evidence that the change improves long-term expectancy.

Optimisation Priorities

Focus on improving expectancy by:

* Filtering lower-quality trade setups.
* Increasing average winning trade size where statistically justified.
* Reducing unnecessary losing trades.
* Improving reward-to-risk selection.
* Improving confidence scoring.
* Improving trend and momentum confirmation.
* Avoiding weak entries during unfavourable market regimes.
* Improving exit management where historical testing supports better outcomes.
* Removing duplicate or conflicting signals.
* Improving consistency rather than simply increasing trade frequency.

The system should favour higher-quality opportunities over a larger number of trades.

Safety Constraints

Before applying any modification:

* Analyse the expected impact.
* Check for dependency conflicts.
* Check for regressions.
* Validate compatibility with every module.
* Reject any optimisation that reduces robustness or creates instability.

If an optimisation cannot be proven beneficial through testing, do not implement it.

Validation Process

After every accepted optimisation:

Run a complete validation suite including:

* Full historical backtesting.
* Walk-forward validation.
* Out-of-sample testing.
* Monte Carlo simulations (where supported).
* Stress testing across different market conditions.
* Performance consistency checks.
* Regression testing.
* Alert verification.
* Signal verification.
* Risk management verification.
* Error logging review.

Performance Report

Produce a report comparing:

* Profit Factor
* Win Rate
* Average Win
* Average Loss
* Expectancy
* Maximum Drawdown
* Sharpe Ratio (if available)
* Number of Trades
* Average Holding Time
* Annualised Return
* Risk-adjusted Return

Include both Before and After values.

Acceptance Criteria

Only keep modifications if they satisfy all of the following:

* No existing functionality is broken.
* Alerts continue functioning correctly.
* Risk controls remain intact.
* No increase in hidden failure modes.
* No material increase in drawdown unless compensated by a statistically significant improvement in expectancy.
* Overall system stability is maintained or improved.
* Profit factor improves with statistically meaningful evidence rather than random variation.

If any optimisation fails these requirements, automatically revert that change.

Final Deliverables

When complete, provide:

1. A summary of every accepted optimisation.
2. Any rejected optimisations and why they were rejected.
3. Before vs After performance metrics.
4. Test results for every validation stage.
5. Confirmation that all alerts, trading logic, logging, reporting, and safety systems continue to operate correctly.
6. Confirmation that the system remains clean, efficient, stable, and production-ready.

The final objective is a stronger long-term trading system, not simply a higher backtest result. Optimisations must prioritise robustness, repeatability, and statistically validated improvements over curve-fitting.
